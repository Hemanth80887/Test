History

Enzo Ferrari

Ferrari, the iconic Italian sports car manufacturer, has a rich history deeply rooted in motorsport. Founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1929 as the Scuderia Ferrari racing team, the company evolved to produce road cars in 1947, starting with the 125 S. Ferrari's legacy is marked by its racing prowess, elegant designs, and enduring status as a symbol of luxury and performance.
Enzo Ferrari, initially a race car driver, founded Scuderia Ferrari in 1929 to manage racing teams for Alfa Romeo. The team competed in various races, with Ferrari himself driving for them.
