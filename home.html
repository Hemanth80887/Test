Ferruccio Lamborghini: Before venturing into the automotive industry, Ferruccio Lamborghini was a successful entrepreneur with businesses in tractors and heating/cooling systems.

Motivation: Lamborghini's desire to build sports cars was fueled by his personal dissatisfaction with the quality and performance of some existing sports cars, including those from Ferrari.

Early Models: The company's first model, the 350 GT, debuted at the Turin Auto Show in 1963. This model featured a lightweight design and a powerful V12 engine.

Miura: The legendary Miura, introduced in the mid-1960s, became a symbol of Lamborghini's innovation and performance with its low profile, transverse mid-engine configuration, and impressive top speed.

Growth and Evolution: Lamborghini has grown into a globally recognized brand, known for its high-performance super sports cars, and is now part of the Volkswagen Group.
